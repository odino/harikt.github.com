Getting Bored of Seeing PHP as a Templating Language

May 27th, 2012

Are you also getting bored of seeing PHP as a templating language ? Why I started to love templating language like django .

The index.html

The layout.html

Template inheritance https://docs.djangoproject.com/en/dev/topics/templates/#id1 http://stackoverflow.com/questions/946627/how-to-implement-template-inheritance-like-django-in-php5 I am not just mentioning placing a require statement. What about placing blocks anyway anywhere ? . It will get rendered in the way its defined in layout.html . May be we will use ob_start() and ob_stop() with PHP ? .

Named Block End-Tags

http://jinja.pocoo.org/docs/templates/#named-block-end-tags

May be everyone mentions we can comment the last statement. But this is not often done by every people :( . Hard to find the real closing tag convert to the new design.

Loop Cylce

http://jinja.pocoo.org/docs/templates/#for

Is this the way you do with PHP? Or better than this ?

May be we will create a partial for the cycle. But everyone will not go creating partials ( and we may want to create each time partials for different websites or copy and paste a common partials ), and when we want to change something in the template it makes stuffs hard to find the end tags. I agree when the editor supports highlighting then its ok. But what will happen when people started to use alternative syntax ?

I have not seen the syntax highlighting when the tags changes to something like above for Netbeans ( May be another plugin is there, I have not checked ) . What about other editors? Have you noticed any sort of highlights ? May be we can say PHP itself is templating language.

Consider we are making a product like blogger. How much easy is it when we have a sandbox where php tags will never get executed, and how easy than not using xml for end users ?. I have played with some Smarty : I don’t love smarty for its not a PSR standard code. Dwo : The further development of Dwoo has stopped. Mustache : Two Mustache implementations for PHP are there . One is https://github.com/bobthecow/mustache.php and other by Mathew Weierophinney https://github.com/weierophinney/phly_mustache in PHP 5.3 . Twig : A fork of Armin Ronacher , later heavily modified by Fabien Potencier http://twig.sensiolabs.org/ . Fabien already have a wonderful article Template Engines in PHP What I understood playing with Mustache is, for template inheritance you want to first render the subview and then later pass the content to the layout. But for twig , you can use the extends tag and render the current view.

Latte : Have you heard about Nette project ? I was looking Nette ‘s templating engine and if that comes as a standalone component ( and following PSR ) for David Grudl has already mentioned it. It seems some more beautiful ? :-) http://doc.nette.org/en/default-macros , http://doc.nette.org/en/templating#toc-latte .
